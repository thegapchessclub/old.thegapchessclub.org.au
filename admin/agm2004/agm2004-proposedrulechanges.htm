AGM 2004 proposed tournament rule changes

The following three proposals are to be considered by club members at the Annual General Meeting of The Gap Chess Club Inc. which will be held at The Gap State High School on 3 December 2004.

 

Note: Proposals 1 to 4 relate specifically to the incremental time controls used in the Flood Cup (rule 13) and the Allegro (rule 3).

 

Motion 1 – Proposed by Brian Willcock (Seconded by Andrew Robinson)

That the evil utilisation of increments in our tournaments be dispensed with and suitable time controls be substituted

 

 

Motion 2 – Proposed by Brian Willcock (Seconded by Andrew Robinson)

That if the bestial system of increments is retained (ie if motion 1 is not successful) increments come into play at the end or near end of the regular time specified, thus avoiding players being able to save up time and in some cases end with more time available than when they started.

 

 

Motion 3 – Proposed by Brian Willcock (Seconded by Andrew Robinson)

That if motion 2 is rejected then increments should be arranged according to the Bronstein rather than the Fischer system, which would mean that increments could not be amassed.

 

 

Motion 4 – Proposed by Brian Willcock (Seconded by Andrew Robinson)

That in the event of the passing of one of the above motions a committee of interested parties decide on the necessary alterations to the existing time controls.

 

 

Motion 5 – Proposed by Brian Willcock (Seconded by Andrew Robinson)

That the St David’s Day tournament continue its experiments and not be bound by any of the above motions – with decisions left to the mystic Druidic conclave already in existence.

 

 

Motion 6 – Proposed by Andrew Robinson (Seconded by Stuart Wilson)

Present rules for the Maiden Trophy:

 

Rule 2 No member shall be eligible to enter this tournament if he/she has already won a tournament of The Gap Chess Club.

Rule 3 No member shall be eligible to enter this tournament if prior to joining the club he/she had an ACF/ELO rating in excess of 1400.

 

Proposal

 

Revoke rules 2 and 3 and replace with:

 

Rule 2 No member shall be eligible to enter this tournament if he/she has already won (or come equal first in) any of the following tournaments:

  • Flood Cup
  • One Hour
  • Allegro
  • Lightning
  • Maiden.

 

 

Motion 7 – Proposed by Andrew Robinson (Seconded by Brian Willcock)

Present rules for the Lightning:

 

Rule 1 The format of this tournament shall be round-robin. If there are 14 entries or less, each round shall consist of FOUR games against each opponent. If there are more than 14 entries each round shall consist of TWO games against each opponent, and/or qualifying stages may be used.

 

Proposal

 

Rule 1 The format of this tournament shall be round-robin. Each round shall consist of the following number of games against each opponent:

(i) 8 entries or less: FOUR games

(ii) from 9 to 16 entries: TWO or FOUR games (at the discretion of the DOP)

(iii) 17 entries or more: TWO games

 

 

Motion 8 – Proposed by Andrew Robinson (Seconded by Tony Howes)

Present rules for the One Hour:

 

Rule 3 Time limit: 60 minutes per player to complete all moves.

 

Proposal

 

Rule 3 Rate of Play: 60 minutes, plus 10 extra seconds per move (from move one).
