Report – 2 March 2018

 

St David’s Day Tournament

 

Martin Laizans became the 14th winner of the St David’s Day Tournament in its 15th edition.

Only Brian Willcock has won more than once, fitting since he provided the inspiration for the tournament to start back in 2004.

A list of this tournament’s winners can be found here.

 

Three players won seven of their nine games in a ten player round-robin, but the handicap system allowed Martin a clear margin of victory.

Martin only lost twice, to Andrew and Tony who were the two other players to win seven.

 

Behind Martin, the next six finished within half a point of each other, validating the complicated method of handicap calculations by the tournament director.

 

Metin provided refreshments again and also put up a solid showing in the tournament.

Returning overseas the next day, the club wished him well on his journey.

Metin’s friend Lachlan, a Park Road Chess regular, joined Metin at the club tonight and played with credit.

 

The club welcomed Chesley Cutler on his first night.

His winning chances improved as he became accustomed to the Bronstein blitz time controls (3min+3sec) and he should provide stern competition in the slower time control tournaments following this one.

 

On a Teams Basis, the Non-Welsh had a slight edge over the Welsh in the final count.
