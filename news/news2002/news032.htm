Report – 19 April 2002

 

Preview of the One Hour tournament 2002

This year has seen the return of many old faces to The Gap Chess Club. Earlier in the year we saw George Flitcroft-Smith come back, winning the Lightning and performing well in the Flood Cup. Recently Ray Adams came back, often playing a friendly game at the slower time controls. Tonight saw more players exercising the right of return: Mark Wardle, who has not been a regular at the club since the late 1980s, and Jack Badenhorst & his son. Together with this year’s new members (among others, John Curran & Garry Jarvis) it would seem that the One Hour could have an entry of 15 to 20, depending on the number of junior entries. If the entry is over 14, it is likely the tournament will be run on a Swiss format. However the attactions of the round-robin format mean that the possibility of running it as a 13 or 15 round tournament have not been entirely ruled out. This option may be feasible with the quicker 1 hour guillotine finish time control (it certainly would not be for the Flood Cup!!).

 

 

Flood Cup 2002

 

Results:

Peter Hiller 1 Brian Willcock 0

Stuart Wilson 0 Doug Foster 1

Harvey Quaresmini 1 James Hiller 0

 

Highlights from tonight’s 3 games:

- Brian Willcock maintained his shockinng run of form. Peter continues his good form from last week when he almost toppled Gabriel;

– Doug Foster managed victory in one of the club’s oldest rivalries;

– James maintained his record, but keeps improving.

 

Brian’s game finished prematurely after a blunder at move 35 conceded a two move mate, however after the game it was demonstrated to everyone’s satisfaction that white’s extra pawn on the b file would be enough to ensure victory with correct play.
