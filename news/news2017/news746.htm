Report – 17 February 2017

 

Lightning Tournament

 

Tonight’s concluding evening to this tournament witnessed many upsets, very different from last Friday when there were few.

 

Of the leading players from last week, all except Andrew lost three games or more tonight, and this was the determining factor in deciding the outcome of the tournament.

 

Martin only lost once last week, but lost his first two games tonight.

After further losses in Rounds 6 and 7, it was a good result to hang on to third place with wins in the final two rounds.

 

In Round Two, Joshua lost to Brian when the latter claimed a win on an illegal move by his opponent.

Further losses his father Tony and Cameron in the next two rounds put paid to his prospects, and even a win against Andrew in Round 6 was not enough.

 

Cameron had an odd night, winning his first four games to have an outside chance of winning the tournament.

However he lost in Round 7 against Andrew in a game he dominated until near the end, blundering in time trouble.

This started the rot, and Cameron following his four straight wins with four straight losses to finish in fourth place.

 

Tony Morris laboured having to play left-handed, a greater liability in this short form of the game where hand-eye coordination is a greater factor.

Despite this, Tony scored 5 wins and losses to the equal second best result on the night, and achieve fourth placing overall.

 

Adam Morris won in Round Four against Tony Howes, probably the biggest upset result in this tournament.

 

Brian Willcock had a greatly improved showing, winning four of his eight games, all of them on merit!

 

Andrew’s 7 wins from 8 games over the board tonight were not as convincing as the scorecard might suggest:

  • Martin – won on an early resignation, perhaps premature, when Martin had a bishop trapped for which he could have scored two pawns as compensation.
  • Adam – won on time when Adam had a overwhelming winning position
  • Brian – won when Brian failed to observe check and his opponent claimed a win for an illegal move
  • Tony M. – won over the board on merit (a rarity!)
  • Joshua – a loss in a close endgame, which Joshua would have won if Andrew’s flag had not fallen
  • Cameron – won a lost game when his opponent blundered a winning position in acute time trouble
  • Rex – won on time when Rex had a overwhelming winning position
  • Tony H. – won on time in a game where Andrew had the better position, but both players blundered in acute time trouble, and the result could have gone either way.
