Report – 9 July 2004

 

One Hour tournament

Round 6 results:

Brian Willcock 1 Stuart Wilson 0

Stuart Holt 0 George Flitcroft-Smith 1

Harvey Quaresmini 0 Luthien Russell 1 (forfeit)

James Hiller v Marty Chadwick (postponed)

Tony Howes v Ross Mills (postponed)

John Curran 0 Andrew Robinson 1

Peter Hiller ½ Debbie Jenkins ½

 

Round 12 result:

Ross Mills 1 Marty Chadwick 0

 

Five games were played tonight over the board. Harvey didn’t show up, so his game was awarded to Luthien without a move being made.

 

Brian achieved two from two!! He gave away a whole rook, but Stuart W still found the moves that would elude most of us to lose the unlosable.

 

George won in a game that should have ended as a draw when Stuart H had the option of knight for two pawns to force the draw. Unfortunately Stuart ran out of time, and his plea for a draw with four seconds remaining could not be upheld as George still had winning chances. The game proceeded and George duly found the winning moves.

 

Andrew found a combination that won a piece as the endgame was approaching, after which John ensured his own downfall with timid play while queens were still on the board. Debbie had two rooks and several pawns versus a rook and pawn but gave away a silly stalemate by touching a rook that she would better have left well alone! She said something to the effect that she will never, never, never, ever, ever do that again.

 

Marty pushed Ross but his position crumbled in the end thus avoiding a final five minute flurry.

 

In non-tournament news, the club saw the return of Joe Tarnawski who dropped in for a night of chess. He played Ray Adams in a long game – Ray gleefully claimed a draw on repitition when he had the chance. After this he played Andrew, then George at lightning before being last out the door just after midnight. Joe is slowing down a bit from his younger days but still plays a steady game that can only be defeated with concentration – anything short of this and you face a massacre!
