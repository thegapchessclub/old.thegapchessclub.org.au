Report – 19 February 2010

 

Prize Night Tournament - Handicap Lightning

 

Prize Night

The night commenced with the presentation of prizes to tournament and trophy winner for 2009. Stuart Wilson was on hand again to hand out the prizes to the prize winners from last year, which were quite well distributed.

 

Handicap Lightning Tournament

For the first time in ten years there were less than 12 entrants and so the tournament was run as a single group round-robin event.

 

A handicap system was used, based on players’ ACF ratings. 1500+ players received a zero handicap (‘playing off scratch’), 1400-1499 would have received a ½ point handicap (but there were none), 1300-1399 1 point, Greg received 2.5 points and Stuart was given a nominal rating of 750 and thus received a 4.0 point handicap. Before each game the difference between the 2 players’ handicaps is calculated, with the lower rated player having 5 minutes in the game to make all moves. The higher rated player is allocated 5 minutes minus the handicap difference (but must have at least one minute). For example, if the difference is 1.5 then the higher rated player will receive 3.5 minutes to make their moves, the lower rated player 5 minutes.

 

The tournament was decided in the last game in which Andrew beat Tony.
