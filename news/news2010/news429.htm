Report - 26 February 2010

 

St David's Day

 

Barbarian massacre of handicapped Welsh

Playing with a massive handicap advantage of 5.4 points the Barbarians easily accounted for the Welsh tonight atoning for last yearâ€™s loss. Such was the margin of the victory that before the last round in which all three Welsh players won, the margin was 7.4 points. The Welsh did have a moral victory however by pipping the Barbarians on the Points total before the handicap was added.

 

The tournament

Mark triumphed with a couple of early wins to maintain the tournaments record of no players winning the individual prize twice. When receiving the leek from Brian he let out a suitable barbarian yell which impressed all who witnessed it.

 

The tournament was decided by the last game to finish. Gary made a false move against Andrew in a pawn endgame which gave away a certain win and tournament victory.

 

Nathan lost to Andrew in Round 2 which scuttled his chances. With players in the lower half of the draw playing well, only a perfect record would do for those in the upper half. Tony had a horrible start to the tournament losing his first three games, including against his nemesis Gary Sands. However his draw against Andrew was to prove vital in deciding the tournament. Even with his last round win against the tournament winner Mark he still ended up last (even without handicaps) â€“ surely a first in Gap competitions.
