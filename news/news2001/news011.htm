Report – 14 September 2001

 

One Hour Tournament 2001

 

Results:

Stuart Wilson 1 James Hiller 0

Doug Foster ½ Patrick Tao ½

Chris Yu 1 Peter Hiller 0

Harvey Quaresmini 1 Brian Willcock 0

 

Many suggested that the amazing run of Brian Willcock in the early rounds of this tournament was a fluke. Brian did his best to prove them right. In the following miniature Brian castles queenside to achieve a kingside iniative. But then, as one of the jokers at the club mentioned, he seemed to run out of suicide bombers! We will leave readers to come to their own judgment from the following miniature……

 

Harvey Quaresmini (1417) - Brian Willcock (1320)

1 e4 e5, 2 Nf3 d6, 3 Bc4 Be7, 4 0-0 Bg4, 5 d3 Nc6, 6 c3 h5, 7 h3 Qd7 (BW intended Bxh3 but then forgot about it and moved his Q), 8 hxg4 0-0-0, 9 Ng5 Rf8, 10 gxh5 Nf6, 11 Bxf7 Rxh5, 12 Be6 Rfh8, 13 Bxd7+ Kxd7, 14 Nh3, Rh6, 15 Bxh6 gxh6 black resigns 1-0

 

The long awaited Round 7 clash between Stuart Wilson and James Hiller has finally occurred. Despite James withdrawing from the tournament, the game went ahead to see if James could break his 15 game losing streak. Alas it was not to be, and this was not helped by a free knight sacrifice at move 4. Stuart played his customary stonewall, and then proceeded to overuse his queen. One felt that if James had managed to trap the white queen the story may have finished differently…. (but then again!…)
