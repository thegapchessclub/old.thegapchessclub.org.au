Queensland Chess 1968

 

There was no mention of The Gap Chess Club in the 1966 to 1968 editions of Chess in Australia.

However, as there were also no articles on the Singer Cup, it is still quite possible that The Gap Chess Club existed at this time.

 

 

The following articles appeared in Chess in Australia  (September 1968, page 110).

 

CITY OF BRISBANE CHAMPIONSHIPS

At the finish of the City of Brisbane Championship, John Myers led Max Salm by ½ point to win the title.

Salm was unexpectedly beaten by Ian Gillies, which was his only loss, and he also conceded a draw to C.H. Lucovich.

Myers lost to Salm and won the rest.

Full results: J. Myers 8; M Salm 7½; I. Gillies 6½; C. Lucovich 5½; A. Vilums 5; M. Dyer 4½; I. Case 4; M. Craven 3.

 

The B Section of this event provided good tournament practice for some of our juniors and was won by J. Watson of Sunnybank High School.

Mrs. Zile tied with J. Hackett, the Qld Junior Champion 1967 for second place.

Results: J. Watson 6½; Mrs. Zile & J. Hackett 5; G. Ingram 4; D. Richardson & G. Grassie 3½; A. Schonheimer, D.J. Lewis & M. Grieco 3; J. Mott 1½.

 

QUEENSLAND CHAMPIONSHIP

The Queensland Championship started on August 10 with an entry of 18.

The ‘bus and tram strike, still on after a week, failed to deter a good entry.

 

INTER-CLUB CORRESPONDENCE LEAGUE

To partly overcome the difficulty of arranging matches and tournaments in this large yet sparsely populated State,
and with the idea of bringing Clubs closer together in a competitive spirit, the C.A.Q. has started an Inter-Club Correspondence League.

 

Eight clubs have entered, divided as follows:

Division A: Brisbane, B.P.C.C., Peninsula (Redcliffe), Southport

Division B: Toowoomba, Townsville, Yeppoon, Longreach

 

SOUTHPORT CHESS CLUB

At long last there is a chess club again on the South Coast.

The Southport Club was formed two months ago and meets on Thursdays, 7-11pm at the Methodist Church Hall, 98 Minnie Street.

President and Secretary, B. Green, 100 Minnie Street, Southport.

 

LONGREACH CHESS CLUB

Mr. Alan Thomas is a spur to chess activities wherever he goes.

Results of the “A” Championship: B. Green 5-0; A. Thomas 4-1; H. Baumann 3-2.

The “B” grade tournament was won by G. Lenton 10-1, a High School student; W. Spence second.

 

The club is running a Problem Competition and meets for play in the State Primary School, Wednesdays.

 

BUNDABERG CHESS CLUB

At a special meeting of the Club, Mr O’Rourke was elected as the new President and John Harris, a strong young player as Secretary-Treasurer.

Re-organisation is planned to ensure a good future for the Club.
