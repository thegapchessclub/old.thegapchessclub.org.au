Singer Cup 1939

 

The following article appeared in The Courier-Mail  (Monday 15 May 1939, page 12).

 

Revising Chess Teams Competition

Delegates from metropolitan clubs to the Queensland Chess Association have decided to renew competition matches between teams.

The clubs represented were the Brisbane, Morphy, Yeronga, German, Russian, and Main Roads Commission.

Any registered team of five members may participate.

Eight teams are expected to compete for the Singer Cup. Play will commence on June 1.

 

 

 

The following article appeared in The Sunday Mail  (Sunday 4 June 1939, page 23).

 

Singer Chess Contest Opens

Six teams took part took part in the inaugural round of the Singer Cup chess competition.

A. E. Evans had a long struggle with C. Potanin. and the game was drawn. K. Olsen played solid chess to win at board 2 for the young Morphy team.

 

Brisbane No. 1 defeated the Russian team, 4½-½: A. E. Evans ½, v. C. Potanin ½; A. S. Cowdry 1, v. P. Serebriakoff 0; F. Oldfield 1, v. V. Morosoff 0; J. Dunning 1, v. Mrs. Morosoff 0; Clarke 1, v. Mrs. Kirriloff 0.

Morphy 1 won with a clean score: — M Salm 1, v W Blackmore 0; K. Olsen 1, v. J Woodward 0; M. Kay 1, v. E. H. Aust 0; R. Anderson I, v. H. Bourne 0; T. Eaton 1, v. G. J. Little 0.

The following article appeared in The Courier-Mail  (Tuesday 26 September 1939, page 11).

 

Chess Premiership

 

By defeating the Russian team, the Morphy No. 1 team has won the metropolitan chess teams' competition, and with it. the F. G. Singer Cup. The team has never been defeated.

 

Details:

Morphy No. 1 beat Russian team, 3-2; M Salm 0. v. C. Potanin 1: K. Olsen 1, v. P Serebriakoff 0; R. Anderson 1, v. V. Morosoff 0; M. Kay 1. v. Mrs. Morosoff 0; Miss Timmins 0, v. Mrs. Klillloff 1.

Nundah defeated Morphy No. 2, 2½-1½; W. Woodward 0, v. P. Wright 1; A. C. Nlelson ½ v. E. Born ½; J. Little 1. v. S. Shahkovsky 0.
