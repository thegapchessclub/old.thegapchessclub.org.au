Various information about the 1984 Singer Cup

 

A Grade (5 Teams)

Brisbane Club – Thursday 7:00pm – Community Arts Building, Edward St, City – Kerry Corker

Manor Junior – Saturday morning 9:00am – Pancake Manor, Charlotte St, City – Michael Forbes

The Gap Club – meets Friday 7:30pm, The Gap State High School, The Gap – Garry de Jager

Westside – Monday night – Inala State School, Glenalla St, Inala – Mal Beattie

Redcliffe – Wednesday night – Redcliffe Community CTE – Downs St, Redcliffe – Bob Goodwin

 

Format: 3 rounds – each team to play 12 matches between March 7 and August 25.

The scoresheets from The Gap v Rochedale home and away show that Ken Barker played board one and beat F Probets on both occasions. John Granger also won both times against Rochedale while Doug Foster lost twice. Harvey Quaresmini lost in the away leg – the player’s name on the home leg lost and this is no doubt why their name is illegible on the results card!

From Doug Foster’s “President’s Report 1984”:

“The past year has to be one of the best on record; our membership reached 32 making us the 4th largest club in Queensland.
In competition for the first time, we fielded four teams in the Singer Cup. One ”A”, and One “B”, and two “C” grade. John Granger picked up the award for best individual in “B” grade while David Mills and Brian Willcock tied for the same in “C” grade. All teams performed with credit.”
